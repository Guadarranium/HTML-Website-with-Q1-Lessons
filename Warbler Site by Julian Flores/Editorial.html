Alice Guo – The Larger Context

By: Jose Ma Montelibano, The Warbler



                    I’m not a formally trained historian, but my interest in history helps me understand things better when I know their context. This approach, which I believe applies to everyone, is key to understanding the recent hot topic: the case of Bamban Mayor Alice Guo. Her situation is intertwined with larger issues, such as the West Philippine Sea dispute and the controversial POGO operations in the Philippines. Mayor Guo’s questionable citizenship reflects growing Filipino resentment toward China due to its territorial aggression and the corruption linked to POGO.

                    The bigger picture involves not only Mayor Guo’s alleged escape and citizenship but also the historical ties between Chinese Filipinos (Chinoys) and native Filipinos. Almost 30% of Filipinos have Chinese ancestry, and Chinoys play a significant role in controlling the Philippine economy. Understanding this historical context is crucial for Mayor Guo and her supporters as they navigate public perception.

                    While Mayor Guo stands her ground, the Philippine government may seize the opportunity to expose deeper corruption. What began as a probe into her citizenship could reveal a broader pattern of how money corrupts politics. This case could be a window into the corruption in Filipino officialdom, and public outrage could force the government to act.

                    The history of Chinese migration to the Philippines, dating back to centuries of trade, complicates the issue further. The current tension between Filipinos and China, worsened by political allegiances and China’s aggressive tactics, only intensifies the conflict. The case of Mayor Guo exemplifies China’s exploitation of the Philippines' weaknesses, such as poverty and corruption, and serves as a reminder that only by understanding the broader historical context can we grasp the full significance of the situation.
